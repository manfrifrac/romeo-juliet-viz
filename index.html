Romeo and Juliet

A dense web of encounters

Every time the names “Romeo” and “Juliet” appear in Shakespeare’s play, they are connected by an ultra-thin red line. The more you zoom into the text, the more individual relations between the two lovers are revealed.

How it works Each occurrence of “Romeo” is linked to every occurrence of “Juliet”. The lines are drawn in a different random order every time, inviting you to explore the text as a living network instead of a static page.
